<p><p>RF bipolar transistors are classified into two main types: NPN and PNP transistors. NPN transistors are built with a layer of p-type semiconductor between two layers of n-type semiconductor, while PNP transistors have a layer of n-type semiconductor between two layers of p-type semiconductor. NPN transistors are more commonly used in amplification applications, while PNP transistors are typically employed in switching applications. Both types play a crucial role in the RF market, catering to different requirements and functionalities.</p></p>
