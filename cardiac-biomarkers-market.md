<p><p>Cardiac biomarkers play a significant role in diagnosing various heart conditions. The main types of cardiac biomarkers include Troponin, CK-MB, Natriuretic Peptides (such as BNP and NT-proBNP), Myoglobin, and others. Troponin is the most commonly used biomarker for diagnosing heart attacks. CK-MB is specific to heart muscle damage. Natriuretic Peptides help in assessing heart failure. Myoglobin is an early indicator of heart muscle damage. Other cardiac biomarkers provide additional information on various heart conditions and aid in accurate diagnosis and treatment.</p></p>
