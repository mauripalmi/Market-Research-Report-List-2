<p><p>Active Safety Systems focus on preventing accidents before they occur by utilizing technology such as automatic emergency braking, lane departure warning, and adaptive cruise control. These systems help the driver avoid potential hazards and maintain control of the vehicle. On the other hand, Passive Safety Systems come into play after an accident has occurred, such as airbags, seat belts, and crumple zones which are designed to minimize the impact of the collision and protect the occupants of the vehicle. Both types of safety systems work together to enhance overall vehicle safety.</p></p>
