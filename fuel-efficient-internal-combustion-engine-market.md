<p><p>Opposed Piston/Opposed Cylinder Engines have a unique design with two pistons moving in opposite directions in the same cylinder, reducing friction and improving efficiency. Reduced Displacement Engines have smaller cylinder volumes, resulting in better fuel economy without sacrificing performance. Split Cycle Engines separate the four strokes of the combustion cycle into different cylinders, optimizing efficiency. Other types of fuel-efficient internal combustion engines include rotary engines and free-piston engines, each designed to maximize fuel efficiency and reduce emissions.</p></p>
