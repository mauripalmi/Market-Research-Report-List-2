<p><p>Optical Fiber Arrays consist of either 1D or 2D configurations. 1D Fiber Arrays are linear arrangements of fibers used for applications such as optical communication and sensing. On the other hand, 2D Fiber Arrays have fibers arranged in a grid pattern, allowing for more complex optical systems and increased data transmission capacity. Both types of Fiber Arrays are crucial components in various industries including telecommunications, medical imaging, and aerospace, enabling high-speed data transfer and efficient signal processing.</p></p>
