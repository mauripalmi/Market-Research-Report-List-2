<p><p>A freeze drier, also known as lyophilizer, is a device used to remove moisture from products by freezing them and then reducing the pressure to allow the frozen water in the product to sublimate directly from the solid phase to gas phase. This process preserves the product and extends its shelf life, making freeze driers popular in the food, pharmaceutical, and biotechnology industries.</p><p>The freeze drier market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for preserved food products, rising adoption of freeze drying technology in pharmaceuticals for drug stability and long-term storage, and the expanding biotechnology sector. The market is also witnessing a surge in research and development activities to improve freeze drying techniques and equipment.</p><p>Latest trends in the freeze drier market include the development of innovative freeze drying systems with enhanced automation and control features, the integration of IoT technology for remote monitoring and control of freeze drying processes, and the introduction of energy-efficient freeze driers to reduce operational costs. Additionally, there is a growing demand for compact and portable freeze drying equipment for lab-scale and point-of-care applications.</p></p>
