<p><ul><li>Circulating</li><li>Non-Circulating</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Oil baths can be categorized into two main types: circulating and non-circulating. Circulating oil baths involve the constant circulation of oil to maintain a uniform temperature throughout the bath, ensuring consistent heating of samples. On the other hand, non-circulating oil baths rely on the static heating of oil without any circulation. Each type has its own advantages and is selected based on specific requirements such as temperature control precision, sample size, and desired heating rates.</p></p>
