<p><p>Phenolic resins are widely used in the automobile industry for various applications due to their exceptional heat resistance, strength, and chemical resistance. There are two main types of phenolic resin used in automobiles: thermoplastic phenolic resin and thermosetting phenolic resin. Thermoplastic phenolic resins can be melted and reshaped multiple times, making them suitable for applications like interior trim components. On the other hand, thermosetting phenolic resins undergo a chemical change when heated, providing excellent durability and stiffness for applications like brake pads and engine components.</p></p>
