<p><p>Direct semiconductor lasers can be categorized into two main market types based on their power output: High Power and Low Power lasers. </p><p>High Power semiconductor lasers are designed for applications that require high-intensity laser beams, such as cutting, welding, and material processing. These lasers are capable of delivering high levels of power and are ideal for industrial and scientific research purposes.</p><p>On the other hand, Low Power semiconductor lasers are used in applications that require less intense laser beams, such as laser pointers, barcode scanners, and telecommunications. These lasers offer lower power levels and are commonly found in consumer electronics and commercial devices.</p></p>
