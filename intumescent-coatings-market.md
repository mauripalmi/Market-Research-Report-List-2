<p><p>Intumescent coatings are fire-resistant coatings that swell up when exposed to high temperatures, forming a protective layer that insulates the underlying material. There are two main types of intumescent coatings: cellulose-based and hydrocarbon-based. Cellulose-based coatings are derived from natural sources such as wood pulp, while hydrocarbon-based coatings are petroleum-derived. Both types provide passive fire protection by delaying the spread of flames and heat transfer. They are commonly used in buildings, industrial facilities, and transportation infrastructure to improve fire safety.</p></p>
