<p><p>Radiation-cured coatings refer to a type of coating that is cured using either ultraviolet (UV) light or electron beam (EB) technology. UV curing involves the use of UV light to initiate a chemical reaction in the coating, resulting in a fast and efficient curing process. On the other hand, EB curing utilizes high-energy electrons to cure the coating, offering advantages such as high-speed curing and decreased volatile organic compounds emissions. Both UV and EB curing methods are popular choices in various industries for their ability to provide durable and environmentally friendly coatings.</p></p>
