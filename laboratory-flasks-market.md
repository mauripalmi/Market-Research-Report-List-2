<p><p>Laboratory flasks come in various types, each with its own unique design and purpose. Conical flasks are shaped like a cone and are ideal for mixing, heating, and storing liquids. Round-bottom flasks have a rounded bottom for easy swirling and heating of liquids. Florence flasks have a round body and long neck, used for distillation and boiling. Volumetric flasks are precise in measuring liquids. Büchner flasks are used in filtration processes. Other types of flasks include Erlenmeyer flasks, filtering flasks, and boiling flasks, each serving different functions in laboratory settings.</p></p>
