<p><p>Chemical agricultural colorants are categorized into two main types: dyes and pigments. Dyes are soluble colored substances that are used to color liquids, whereas pigments are insoluble colored materials that are used to color solid materials such as plastics, rubber, and paper. Dyes are typically more vivid and transparent, while pigments provide a more opaque and long-lasting color. Both dyes and pigments play a crucial role in the agricultural industry by helping farmers easily identify treated crops, distinguish between different products, and improve overall aesthetics.</p></p>
