<p><p>Dimming glass refers to glass that can change its transparency or opacity based on external stimuli. PDLC (Polymer Dispersed Liquid Crystal) glass uses an electric current to switch between clear and frosted states. Electrochromic glass utilizes a low-voltage electrical charge to control light and heat transmission. Other types of dimming glass in the market include thermochromic glass, which responds to temperature changes, and photochromic glass, which darkens in response to UV light exposure. These technologies offer energy efficiency, privacy, and aesthetics in various applications.</p></p>
