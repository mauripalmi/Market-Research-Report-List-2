<p><p>Lab homogenizers are used in laboratories to mix, emulsify, disperse, and disrupt samples for various research purposes. Two common types of lab homogenizers are bench-top and handheld models. Bench-top homogenizers are stationary machines typically used for larger sample volumes and are designed to be placed on a lab workspace. Handheld homogenizers, on the other hand, are portable devices that offer greater flexibility and convenience for smaller samples. Both types of homogenizers play a crucial role in the scientific research process.</p></p>
